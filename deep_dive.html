~ I. The COIN System & the Card Engine ~

Liberty or Death is the fifth volume in GMT Games' celebrated COIN (counter-insurgency) series, designed by Harold Buchanan and adapting Volko Ruhnke's system from modern conflicts to the American Rebellion of 1775–1783.Rules 1.0 Up to four players take the roles of the British, Patriots, Indians, and French — two Royalist, two Rebellion — each with asymmetric forces, unique commands, and separate victory conditions.Rules 1.5

The engine is the card deck. Cards are revealed one at a time onto a played-card stack, with the next card on the deck simultaneously visible — players always see one card ahead.Rules 2.2 Each Event card displays a sequence of Faction flags across the top: this strict left-to-right ordering determines who may act, in what order, and with what menu of choices.Rules 2.3 Winter Quarters cards, mixed throughout the deck, interrupt play for victory checks and the round of economic and political reckoning.Rules 2.4

The COIN system's central tension is asymmetry. The factions don't share rules, currencies, or even victory conditions — and same-side factions (British+Indians, Patriots+French) are friends but cannot share a win. As the rules note, "When one Faction wins, the other Faction on the same side does not win." This produces alliances of convenience rather than genuine partnership.Rules 1.5.1

~ II. Alignment, Control & Population ~

The Five Levels of Allegiance

Every Province with Population ≥ 1 and every City sits at one of five levels of popular feeling toward the British Crown:Rules 1.6.1 Active Support, Passive Support, Neutral, Passive Opposition, Active Opposition. Spaces with 0 Population — the four Indian Reserve provinces and the West Indies — are always Neutral and may never shift.Rules 1.6.2

A space's contribution to Total Support or Total Opposition is its Population multiplied by 1 for Passive, 2 for Active, per the rule:

Total Support = (2 × Pop in Active Support) + (1 × Pop in Passive Support)
Total Opposition = (2 × Pop in Active Opposition) + (1 × Pop in Passive Opposition) Rules 1.6.3

These totals feed directly into every faction's first victory condition.Rules 7.2 Because Active levels count double, shifting a 2-Population colony from Passive to Active is worth as much as winning a Neutral colony to Passive twice over.

Who Controls a Space?

Control is a tally of pieces, not a token bought with Resources:Rules 1.7

ConditionEffect
Rebellion pieces (Patriot + French) > Royalist piecesRebellion controls.
Royalist pieces > Rebellion pieces, and ≥ 1 British piece presentBritish control.
Indian pieces alone equal or exceed Rebellion pieces, no British presentNobody controls.
Pieces equalNobody controls.
Indian pieces alone cannot establish British Control — they need at least one red British cube present in the space. This is a subtle and frequently-missed point that constrains Indian operations on the frontier.Rules 1.7

The Stacking Limits

No more than two Forts or Villages (from any faction, in any combination) may occupy a single space.Rules 1.4.2 Indian War Parties and Villages may never enter Cities. The West Indies has its own restrictions: only British and French Regulars, British Forts, and French Squadrons may occupy that holding box.Rules 1.3.7, 1.4.2

~ III. Eligibility & the Choice of Action ~

The Sequence of Play track holds each faction's cylinder in one of three positions: Eligible, Ineligible, or — after Winter Quarters — reset to Eligible for all.Rules 2.3.1 The 1st Eligible faction (the leftmost flag among Eligible factions) chooses first; the 2nd Eligible follows.

The Decision Tree

The 1st Eligible may Pass (for +1 R if Patriots/Indians, +2 R if British/French)Rules 2.3.3, execute the Event, or take a Command — with or without an accompanying Special Activity.Rules 2.3.4

The 2nd Eligible's options narrow based on what the 1st did:

1st Eligible did:2nd Eligible may:
Command onlyLimited Command only (one space, no SA)
Command + Special ActivityLimited Command OR execute the Event
EventFull Command with optional Special Activity
PassSame menu, as if they were 1st Eligible
This cascade creates one of the game's central tensions: should the 1st Eligible take an SA — a strong move, but one that opens the door for the 2nd Eligible to grab the Event? Often the answer is "yes, but only if the Event would help my opponent more than it helps me to take the SA." Reading the Event flags and shaded/unshaded text is as critical as planning your own move.Rules 5.2

Limited Commands

A Limited Command involves just one space and no Special Activity.Rules 2.3.5 When the command involves moving pieces from multiple origins, all moving pieces must converge on a single destination space. Limited Commands still cost a cylinder — they render the faction Ineligible for the next card.

Adjusting Eligibility

After both factions have acted (or both have Passed), cylinders are repositioned:Rules 2.3.6 Any faction that acted moves to Ineligible; any that did not, remains Eligible. Some Events override this, leaving the executor Eligible or rendering others Ineligible for the next card.

~ IV. Brilliant Strokes & the Treaty of Alliance ~

Each faction begins each scenario with a Brilliant Stroke card — a personal trump held in hand. The French alone begin with two, the second being the famous Treaty of Alliance.Rules 2.3.8

Procedure of the Stroke

A Brilliant Stroke may be played to cancel the current Event card if:

  • The playing faction is Eligible;
  • The 1st Eligible has not yet acted;
  • No Winter Quarters card is showing.

The Brilliant Stroke is placed atop the played Event, and its instructions are executed. The Event beneath is never played.Rules 2.3.8 All factions become Eligible after a Brilliant Stroke resolves — including the faction that played it. The Leader requirement is strict: the Leader must be in a space involved in at least one Limited Command, or the Brilliant Stroke cannot be played.Rules 1.10

The Trumping Hierarchy

A Brilliant Stroke can itself be Trumped — cancelled before it resolves — by another faction's Brilliant Stroke. The hierarchy is:Rules 2.3.8

FactionMay Trump
Treaty of Alliance (French)Any Brilliant Stroke — and cannot itself be Trumped.
IndiansAny other faction's Brilliant Stroke (except Treaty of Alliance).
FrenchPatriots or British.
BritishPatriots only.
PatriotsMay never Trump.

The Treaty of Alliance Threshold

The Treaty of Alliance has a strict precondition: the French must have accumulated more than 15 Available French Regulars + Squadrons/Blockades + Cumulative British Casualties.Rules 2.3.9 This total — called French Preparations — is tracked on the edge track. Until the threshold is met, the Treaty cannot be played and the French are sharply limited.

French War Prep is equal to available French troops, fleets in the West Indies, plus one-half of British casualties. When that reaches 15, the French can actively move troops onto the board and start having some real fun. Before that, they're limited to sneakily helping the Patriots. — Stately Play, Stately Sessions: Liberty or Death, Turn 3
Stately Play's note above describes the Non-player French threshold (which uses ½ CBC), not the player threshold (which uses full CBC).Rules 2.3.9 vs 8.1 If you're playing the French yourself, the entire CBC counts — meaning the Treaty drops sooner the more British you've killed.

Before the Treaty, the French may only execute Roderigue Hortalez et CieRules 3.5.2, French Agent MobilizationRules 3.5.1, the Préparer la Guerre Special ActivityRules 4.5.1, and Events from cards. After the Treaty: Muster, March, Battle, Skirmish, and Naval Pressure all unlock. Without playing the Treaty, the French cannot win.Rules 7.2, 7.3

~ V. The Anatomy of Battle ~

Battle in Liberty or Death is simultaneous and bloody — both sides calculate force, both roll dice, both take losses. The procedure runs through five steps for each Battle space:Rules 3.6

Step 1 — Factional Cooperation

The faction executing the Battle Command makes all decisions for its side, but cannot use allied pieces without payment:Rules 3.6.1

  • French attacking may only use Patriot pieces if Patriots pay.
  • Patriots attacking may only use French pieces if French pay.
  • British attacking may always use Indian pieces and Leader if a War Party is in the space — at no Indian cost.
  • Indians cannot execute a Battle Command. They participate only via the British.

Step 2 — Force Level

Each side calculates its Force Level:Rules 3.6.3

  • Each cube (Regular, Continental, Tory, French Regular) = +1 each.
  • Active Militia or Active War Party = +½ each (rounded down on total).
  • Forts count only for the defender = +1 each.
  • The attacker may choose to activate Underground pieces to raise their count (which they often must do to qualify for the Underground modifier — see below).
  • Tory contribution is capped at the number of British Regulars present.
  • French may join Rebellion attacks if Patriots pay; same in reverse.

Once Force Level is set: divide by 3 (rounding down) to determine the number of D3 dice rolled, capped at 3 dice. If Force Level is 2 or less, no dice are rolled and the result is treated as 0.Rules 3.6.4

Rules 3.6.6

Forts are remarkably powerful on defense: each Fort gives +1 to the attacker's loss level and −1 to the defender's loss level — a 2-point swing per Fort. This is why dislodging a fortified Patriot position is so expensive in cubes.Rules 3.6.5–3.6.6

Step 4 — Removal

Each side must remove pieces totalling at least its modified Loss Level. Regulars, Continentals, and Forts each count as 2 losses; Tories, Militia, War Parties, and Villages count as 1.Rules 3.6.7 Removal order is strictly prescribed:

SideRemoval Order
RoyalistsAlternate British Regulars ↔ Tories, then Active War Parties, then (defender only) Villages, then Forts.
RebellionAlternate French Regulars ↔ Continentals ↔ Active Militia, then (defender only) Forts.
BothUnderground Militia and War Parties are ignored — they cannot be removed.

Rules 3.6.7

Removed Regulars, Continentals, French Regulars, and Tories go to the Casualties box and remain unusable until the Reset Phase of the next Winter Quarters Round.Rules 1.6.4 Forts are immediately returned to Available — but still count toward CBC or CRC. Militia, War Parties, and Villages go straight back to Available.

Step 5 — Win the Day

If the loser removed at least one cube or Fort, and at least 2 total pieces, and the battle is not in the West Indies, Win-the-Day occurs:Rules 3.6.8

  • The winner is the side that lost the fewest pieces (not loss points). Defender wins ties.
  • Shift Support/Opposition by half the loser's pieces removed (rounding down), maximum 3 levels. Washington doubles this to a max of 6 if Rebellion wins.
  • Excess shifts can spill into adjacent spaces (British or Patriots).
  • If Rebellion wins: Patriots get a free Rally in one eligible space, and French may relocate any Blockades from the battle City to another City.
Win-the-Day rewards not losing, not necessarily winning by elimination. A defender who survives with one Underground Militia after a brutal exchange can still take the propaganda victory if the attacker lost more pieces. This is one reason Underground status is so prized — it shelters pieces from removal and from the piece count that determines Win-the-Day.Rules 3.6.8

~ VI. Naval Intervention & Blockades ~

French Naval Intervention (FNI) tracks French maritime commitment to the war. Its level determines how many Blockades are placed on British Cities.Rules 1.9 Before the Treaty of Alliance is played, FNI must remain at 0; no Blockades may be placed at all. After the Treaty, both the British (lowering) and French (raising) may move the level through Naval Pressure.Rules 4.2.3, 4.5.3

The Crushing Effects of a Blockade

A Blockade on a City has five direct effects:Rules 1.9

  • No British Muster of Regulars in that City, nor in an adjacent Colony — unless that Colony is also adjacent to another non-Blockaded City.
  • No British Garrison to or from that City.
  • No British March to that City or to Provinces adjacent to it (unless destination is directly adjacent to origin).
  • The City's Population counts as 0 for Support calculations and Resource phase income.
  • British Garrison Command unavailable entirely if FNI is at level 3.Rules 3.2.2

Available Squadrons sit in the West Indies holding box. Each Squadron present increases the British Loss Level by 1 in any Battle there — a brutal scaling factor for the West Indies fight.Rules 1.9, 3.6.5

The Squadron / Blockade Flip

The same counter has two sides: Squadron (in the West Indies) and Blockade (on a City). When FNI is raised, the French flip a counter to Blockade and place it on any City. When the British lower FNI via Naval Pressure, they remove a Blockade to the West Indies and flip it back to Squadron.Rules 1.9 During Redeploy, FNI automatically drops by 1 each Winter.Rules 6.5.4

FNI-related Events may appear before the Treaty of Alliance is played. The rule is clear: ignore all FNI impacts and place no Blockades until after the Treaty.Rules 1.9 This includes any Event text that would raise or lower FNI.

~ VII. The Winter Quarters Procession ~

When a Winter Quarters card appears in play, the round halts and the seven phases of the Winter Round resolve in order. Each phase has its own rule section and its own subtleties.Rules 6.0

1. Victory CheckRules 6.1

If any faction has met both its victory conditions with a positive margin, the game ends immediately. The French additionally need the Treaty of Alliance to have been played. If the Final Winter Quarters card has been drawn, the round proceeds through the Support Phase and then game-end victory is calculated by combined margins.Rules 7.2, 7.3

2. SupplyRules 6.2

Each faction checks supply lines for its on-map pieces:

FactionIn Supply When…
BritishIn a space with a British Fort, or in a City with British Control. Out: pay 1 R, shift one toward Active Opposition, or remove cubes.
PatriotsIn a space with a Patriot Fort, or a Colony/City with Rebellion Control. Out: pay 1 R, or remove 1 per 2 units.
FrenchIn a space with a Patriot Fort, or a Colony/City with Rebellion Control. Out: move to nearest Rebellion-Fort space, pay, or return to Available.
IndiansIn a space with a Village, or in an Indian Reserve. Out: pay 1 R, or move to nearest Village.

If no Villages are on the map at all, Indians place one free Village in any Indian Reserve before checking supply.Rules 6.2.1 The French must conduct a free Battle in the West Indies if both French and British pieces are present.Rules 6.2.2

3. ResourcesRules 6.3

FactionIncome
BritishBritish Forts on map + Population of non-Blockaded British-controlled Cities. +5 if British control the West Indies.

PatriotsPatriot Forts + half the number of Rebellion-controlled spaces (excluding the West Indies, rounded down).

4. Support PhaseRules 6.4

British spend on Reward Loyalty: 1 R per shift toward Active Support, in spaces with British Regular + Tory + British Control. Removes Raid/Propaganda markers first, then shifts. Max 2 levels per space.Rules 6.4.1 Patriots spend on Committees of Correspondence: same idea, toward Active Opposition, in Rebellion-controlled spaces with Patriot pieces. Max 2 levels per space.Rules 6.4.2

5. RedeploymentRules 6.5

The Leader Change Summary forces progression: Gage → Howe → Clinton; Rochambeau → Lauzun; Brant → Cornplanter → Dragging Canoe.Rules 6.5.1 French Leaders do not change until after the Treaty. Each faction then redeploys its Leader (Indians first, then French, British, Patriots).Rules 6.5.2 British move pieces from Unavailable per the Scenario's Release Date Schedule.Rules 6.5.3 FNI drops 1 level.Rules 6.5.4

6. DesertionRules 6.6

Remove 1 in 5 Militia, 1 in 5 Continentals, and 1 in 5 Tories from the map (rounding down). Indians choose the first Militia and first Continental to desert; Patriots pick the rest. French choose the first Tory; British pick the rest.Rules 6.6.1–6.6.2

7. ResetRules 6.7

Clear all Raid and Propaganda markers. Mark all factions Eligible. Move Casualties to Available. Flip all Militia and War Parties to Underground. Reveal the next card. Resolve the Winter Quarters card's own Event text if applicable.

The Final Winter Quarters card omits Phases 5, 6, and 7 — Redeployment, Desertion, and Reset.Rules 6.4.3 The game ends after Support Phase with combined victory margins.

~ IX. Victory: Margins & the Final Reckoning ~

Victory is checked twice — during each Winter Quarters Round (with strict positive thresholds) and at game-end after the Final Winter Support Phase (with combined margins).Rules 7.0

In-Round Victory Check (Strict)

Both victory conditions must have positive margins. The French additionally need to have played the Treaty of Alliance.Rules 7.2

FactionCondition ICondition II
BritishSupport − Opposition > 10CRC > CBC
PatriotsOpposition − Support > 10(Forts + 3) > Villages
FrenchOpposition − Support > 10CBC > CRC (plus TOA played)
IndiansSupport − Opposition > 10(Villages − 3) > Forts

End-of-Game Victory (Combined Margins)

If the Final Winter Quarters Support Phase completes with no winner, both conditions for each faction are summed into a single victory margin — and the highest margin wins, even if negative.Rules 7.3 The French still must have played the Treaty.

FactionTotal Margin Formula
British(Support − Opposition) + (CRC − CBC)
Patriots(Opposition − Support) + (Forts + 3 − Villages)
French(Opposition − Support) + (CBC − CRC); TOA required
Indians(Support − Opposition) + (Villages − 3 − Forts)

Ranking & Ties

When a single faction wins an in-round check, its same-side ally takes 2nd; the highest-margin enemy takes 3rd; the other enemy 4th.Rules 7.1 If the Treaty of Alliance was never played, the French finish last regardless of margin or Non-player status.Rules 7.1 Ties break in order: Non-players, Patriots, British, French, Indians.

The asymmetry of victory conditions is the heart of the game's drama. The British and Indians share the Support-Opposition direction, but the British want CRC > CBC while the Indians want Villages > Forts. They can be on the same side and still see one another's progress as threatening — a Patriot Fort that the British shrug at might cost an Indian player the game.Rules 7.2

~ X. Strategic Counsels by Faction ~

Drawn from the considered judgement of designers, reviewers, and players, with the rule clauses that support each maxim.

B

The British — Counsel of the Crown

Flexibility by sea; weight by land. Lose neither.
First Principle — Master the Cities

The British Resource phase income is the number of British Forts plus the Population of non-Blockaded British-controlled Cities.Rules 6.3.1 Every City you lose is a Resource hit and a Support hit. GMT's own publisher description captures the British dilemma:

As the British, you have to deal with an Insurrection across a massive region. With control of the seas (at least until the French arrive), you have extreme flexibility and can move across the coast and cities at will. You will muster Tories to support your efforts. You can control any space you choose, but you cannot answer every threat on the map.— GMT Games, Liberty or Death product description
Second Principle — Tories Are Your Force Multiplier

Tories Muster cheaply (1 R per space) and can be placed in any City or Colony at non-Active-Opposition adjacent to British Regulars or Forts.Rules 3.2.1 In Battle, Tories count as cubes up to the number of Regulars, swelling your Force Level without spending Regulars.Rules 3.6.3 Crucially, Reward Loyalty requires both a Regular and a Tory in the space — Tories are not optional for political work.Rules 3.2.1, 6.4.1

Third Principle — Mid-Round Reward Loyalty Is Underused

Reward Loyalty can be folded into a Muster Command in mid-campaign, not just during Winter Support.Rules 3.2.1 Unlike the Winter version (capped at 2 levels per space), the mid-Command version has no shift cap — but you pay one Resource per level plus per Propaganda/Raid marker present. With Gage, the first such shift is free.Rules 1.10

Fourth Principle — Manage CBC Carefully

Your second victory condition requires CRC > CBC.Rules 7.2 Battles that pile losses onto your worst metric are bad even when you "win." Consider whether a Skirmish (which still creates British casualties only when sacrificing Regulars 1-for-2) or a Garrison-displace is cheaper than a Battle Command.Rules 3.2.2, 4.2.2

Fifth Principle — Suppress FNI Before the Treaty

Use Naval Pressure to add Resources pre-Treaty (+1d3 R).Rules 4.2.3 Post-Treaty, Naval Pressure removes Blockades. The Treaty threshold counts your CBC, so heavy losses speed the French entry.Rules 2.3.9

P

The Patriots — Counsel of the Continental Congress

You cannot win on the field. You must win on the map.
First Principle — Spread, Then Concentrate

GMT's own player guidance is direct on this point:

As the Patriots, you initially aren't powerful enough to counteract the British Army. You will need to pick your battles and initially spread the Militia to key areas. Over time you can train a force of Continentals to take on the British Regulars. Until then, Rabble-rouse and work with the Indians… er, the French.— GMT Games, Liberty or Death product description

Stately Play adds a tactical refinement for the campaign scenario: don't get sucked into a New England slugging match with the British.

Focus on not getting trapped in the north-east, duking it out with the British. Ensure good presence in the south, and be ready to push back against the Indians.— Stately Play, Stately Sessions: Liberty or Death, Turns 1 and 2
Second Principle — Underground Militia Is Your Shield

Underground Militia cannot be removed in Battle, do not count toward Win-the-Day piece tallies, and grant the +1 Underground Loss Level modifier to your side.Rules 3.6.5–3.6.8 Activate only when you must — and the Rally Command's "pull adjacent Militia and flip all Underground" option is one of the most efficient refresh mechanisms in the game.Rules 3.3.1

Third Principle — Forts Are Strategic Real Estate

A Patriot Fort is worth 1 toward your second victory condition.Rules 7.2 But it also: doubles your Rally output in the space (Population + Forts Militia), serves as a supply base for Continentals and French Regulars, and adds 2 swing in any defensive Battle there. Forts placed in the interior survive longer than coastal Forts within range of British movement.Rules 3.3.1, 6.2.1, 3.6.5–3.6.6

Fourth Principle — Persuasion Is a Bank

Persuasion in 3 Rebellion-controlled Colonies/Cities yields +3 R and activates 1 Underground Militia per space — and places Propaganda markers that future British Reward Loyalty must pay to clear.Rules 4.3.1 Coupled with Rabble-Rousing (which also places Propaganda), you can shape an entire region's political and economic future in two turns.Rules 3.3.4

Fifth Principle — Watch the Forts-Villages Race

Your victory condition is Patriot Forts + 3 > Indian Villages. The Indian condition is the inverse: Villages − 3 > Patriot Forts. If the Indians out-build you and you can't keep pace with Forts, your Opposition margin may win the first condition but lose you the second — and the game.Rules 7.2–7.3 Skirmish and Partisans both have rules for removing Villages when no other Royalist pieces are present.Rules 4.3.2, 4.3.3

I

The Indians — Counsel of the War Chiefs

Overlooked is a kind of strength.
First Principle — You Are Always In the Game

The Players' Aid notes a recurring dynamic worth internalizing:

I have found that most of the time the Patriots and French, as well as their own ally the British, just ignore and overlook the Indians allowing them to always be in the game at the end reckoning.— The Players' Aid, COIN Workshop: Indian Faction

Your lack of profile is your protection. Trade Resources with the British, Raid Patriot frontier colonies, and quietly build your Villages while the major factions claw at each other.

Second Principle — The Indian Reserve Is Your Sanctuary

The first Gather in an Indian Reserve Province is free.Rules 3.4.1 The first destination of a March from an Indian Reserve is free.Rules 3.4.2 Indians defending in an Indian Reserve get −1 to Defender Loss Level.Rules 3.6.5 Indian War Parties retreat into Reserves for supply at no cost.Rules 6.2.1 Build your Village base here — the four Reserves can hold up to 8 Villages safely.Rules 1.4.2

Third Principle — Raid Pays for Itself

Raid hits up to three Provinces for 1 R each, shifting Opposition toward Neutral and dropping Raid markers that the Patriots must clear via Committees of Correspondence (a 1 R cost each).Rules 3.4.4, 6.4.2 Pair Raid with Plunder where War Parties exceed Rebellion pieces, and you transfer Population-equal Resources from Patriots to yourself.Rules 4.4.3

Fourth Principle — Trade Is Both Resource and Lever

Trade can transfer British Resources to you with no risk, or generate +1 R if the British decline. The British rarely give freely — extract a promise of Scout or favourable Battle support in return.Rules 4.4.1 The Players' Aid emphasizes:

Between the Trade and Plunder Special Activity, an active Indian player should have no problem staying flush with Resources and can be a real source of difficulty for the Patriot player.— The Players' Aid, COIN Workshop: Indian Faction
Fifth Principle — Scout Is a British Loan

Scout moves War Parties + at least 1 British Regular into an adjacent Province, then lets the Indians decide how the British Skirmish there.Rules 3.4.3 The Indians benefit, but so does the British player (who can engage Patriots without spending a Battle Command). Use it especially to project into Rebellion-controlled border colonies.

Sixth Principle — Watch the British, Not Just the Patriots

You lose if the British win and out-margin you, even though you are allies.Rules 1.5.1 If the British look poised for victory, you may need to throttle their Support gains or refuse to Trade — bizarre as it sounds, the British are your second-greatest threat after Patriot Forts.

F

The French — Counsel of Versailles

First the gold; then the powder; then the Marquis.
First Principle — Build the Treaty Threshold

Treaty of Alliance requires Available French Regulars + Squadrons/Blockades + CBC > 15.Rules 2.3.9 Every Préparer la Guerre Special Activity moves either 3 Regulars to Available or a Squadron to the West Indies — each contributes to the threshold.Rules 4.5.1 Pace yourself: drop the Treaty when you have enough Regulars to make a meaningful first Muster, but not so late that the deck buries it.

Second Principle — Roderigue Hortalez et Cie Is the Patriots' Lifeline

Every French Resource spent transfers that amount + 1 to the Patriots.Rules 3.5.2 The Players' Aid emphasizes the symbiosis:

The Patriots need the French because they are desperately underfunded and the French can, using their French Agent Mobilization (to place 2 Militia or 1 Continental in Quebec, New York, New Hampshire or Massachusetts) and Roderigue Hortalez Et Cie (to increase Patriot resources to provide means to Rally, March and Battle) Commands to aid the rebellion while biding their time until they can play the infamous Treaty of Alliance.— The Players' Aid, review of Liberty or Death

Before the Treaty, every Patriot you strengthen is killing British casualties — which feeds your Treaty threshold and your second victory condition (CBC > CRC).

Third Principle — French Agent Mobilization in New England

French Agent Mobilization places 2 Militia or 1 Continental in Quebec, New York, New Hampshire, or Massachusetts.Rules 3.5.1 Seed Patriot strength in the north so that when the Treaty arrives and you Muster Regulars, you Muster into Rebellion-controlled spaces — which French Muster requires.Rules 3.5.3

Fourth Principle — FNI as Economy and Denial

Post-Treaty, FNI feeds your Resource phase directly (FNI level + non-British City Population, +5 for West Indies control).Rules 6.3.4 Each Blockade also: zeros that City's Population for Support and British income, kills British Muster of Regulars in adjacent Colonies, halts British Garrison through it, and — at FNI 3 — eliminates the British Garrison Command entirely.Rules 1.9, 3.2.2

Fifth Principle — Mind the West Indies

If both French and British pieces are in the West Indies during Winter Quarters, French must conduct a free Battle there.Rules 6.2.2 West Indies Battle has no Win-the-Day shift (it has 0 Population) but it does feed CBC and CRC. Each Squadron present gives British −1 Loss Level there — both attacking and defending.Rules 3.6.5–3.6.6 Use Squadrons not just for placement on the map but as a "fleet in being" for the West Indies fight.

Sixth Principle — Drop the Treaty Even If Imperfect

If the deck threatens to bury the Treaty card behind Winter Quarters, play it sub-optimally rather than miss the window. Without the Treaty, you cannot win at all — neither in-round nor at game-end.Rules 7.1, 7.2, 7.3
