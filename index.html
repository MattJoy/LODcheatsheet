~ The Essentials ~

Four factions vie for the soul of British North America. The Royalists — British & Indians — fight to preserve the Crown's order. The Rebellion — Patriots & French — seek its overthrow. Same-side factions are friends but win separately.

Population & Alignment

Every Province and City of Population ≥ 1 sits at one of five levels: Active Support, Passive Support, Neutral, Passive Opposition, Active Opposition. Active counts double for victory totals. 1.6.1–1.6.3

Control

The Rebellion controls a space when Patriot+French pieces outnumber Royalist pieces. The British control when Royalist pieces are more and at least one British piece is present. 1.7

Resources

Each faction holds 0–50 Resources, paid to execute Commands. Passing the card grants +1 (Patriots/Indians) or +2 (British/French). 1.8, 2.3.3

Eligibility

Factions move in the order shown atop each card. After acting on a card, you sit out the next. Pass to refill the purse and stay Eligible. 2.3.1–2.3.6

Underground vs Active

Militia and War Parties are placed Underground (hidden). They flip Active when battling, marching into a controlled space in force, or being rabble-roused. 1.4.3

Casualties (CBC / CRC)

Cumulative British Casualties and Cumulative Rebellion Casualties are tallied through the whole game on edge tracks. Both feed directly into victory checks. 1.6.4, 7.2

~ The Sequence of Play ~

Cards drive everything. One Event card is in play; the next is revealed atop the deck. Up to two factions act per card, in the order their flags appear.

I

1st Eligible

Pass (+ Resources), execute the Event, or take a Command — with or without a Special Activity. 2.3.4

II

2nd Eligible

Options depend on what the 1st did: if Command only → Limited Command; if Command + SA → LimCom or Event; if Event → full Command with SA. 2.3.4–2.3.5

III

Brilliant Stroke

Each faction holds one (French: two, incl. Treaty of Alliance). Plays as a trump before anyone acts; cancels the current Event. 2.3.8–2.3.9

IV

Winter Quarters

When this card surfaces, swap it in. Check victory, supply, resources, support, redeploy, desertion, reset. 2.4, 6.0

~ Factional Breakdown ~

B
The British
Royalist Side · Red Regulars & Green Tories
Commands
  • Muster — place Regulars in a City or adjacent Colony (1 R per space); place Tories near Regulars/Forts; build a Fort from 3 cubes; or Reward Loyalty to shift Support. 3.2.1
  • Garrison — 2 R total; move Regulars into Cities, then Activate one Militia per 3 cubes; displace Rebellion from one British-controlled City. Unavailable at FNI 3. 3.2.2
  • March — 1 R per destination; Regulars to adjacent spaces, or via Cities; Tories may accompany 1-for-1; Activates Militia where 3+ cubes end. 3.2.3
  • Battle — 1 R per space; engage Rebellion pieces. Indians fight beside you free of charge if War Parties are present. 3.2.4, 3.6.1
Special Activities
  • Common Cause — use War Parties as Tories in March or Battle spaces. They activate. 4.2.1
  • Skirmish — strike Rebellion with Regulars in one non-Battle space; trade 1 Regular for 2 enemy cubes/Militia. 4.2.2
  • Naval Pressure — pre-Treaty: +1d3 R. Post-Treaty: lower FNI by 1 (or +1d3 R if FNI is 0). 4.2.3
Leaders
  • Gage — 1st Reward Loyalty in his space is free.
  • Howe — lower FNI 1 before any British SA.
  • Clinton — Skirmish removes 1 extra Militia in his space.

The Patriots
Rebellion Side · Blue Militia, Continentals, & Forts
Commands
  • Rally — 1 R per space (no Active Support); place Militia, or build a Fort from 2 units. With a Fort present, place extra Militia or pull adjacent Militia in & flip Underground. 3.3.1
  • March — 1 R per destination; move Militia, Continentals (and French if French pays). Continentals near Patriot Forts can replace Militia. 3.3.2

Special Activities
  • Persuasion — in up to 3 Rebellion-controlled Colonies/Cities, activate 1 Underground Militia and gain 1 R each. Place Propaganda. 4.3.1
  • Partisans — in one space with Underground Militia & Royalist pieces (not a Battle space); activate Militia to remove enemy units. 4.3.2
  • Skirmish — Continentals strike British in one non-Battle space. 4.3.3
Leader
  • Washington — doubles Rebellion Win-the-Day shift in his space; –1 to Defender Loss Level when Rebellion is defending there. 1.10

I
The Indians
Royalist Side · Tan War Parties & Villages
Commands
  • Gather — 1 R per Province (free in first Indian Reserve). Not in Active Support/Opposition. Place a War Party, or build a Village from 2 War Parties. With a Village, place extra or pull adjacent War Parties in & flip Underground. 3.4.1
  • March — 1 R per Province destination (first Indian-Reserve origin is free). War Parties only; Provinces only. 3.4.2
  • Scout — 1 R (British also pays 1 R); move War Parties + at least 1 British Regular into adjacent Province; activate all moving War Parties & enemy Militia, then optionally Skirmish. 3.4.3
  • Raid — up to 3 Provinces with Opposition & Underground War Parties; activate one, place a Raid marker, shift Opposition 1 step toward Neutral. 3.4.4
Special Activities
  • Trade — one Province with Underground War Party & Village; British may transfer Resources, or take +1 R yourself & activate one War Party. 4.4.1
  • War Path — strike Rebellion with Underground War Parties anywhere; sacrifice War Parties for kills. 4.4.2
  • Plunder — accompanies Raid only; where War Parties exceed Rebellion pieces, steal Resources from Patriots equal to Population & lose 1 War Party. 4.4.3
Leaders
  • Brant — Skirmish removes 1 extra Militia in space.
  • Cornplanter — War Path removes 1 extra Militia.
  • Dragging Canoe — Gather builds a Village from 1 War Party.

~ Winter Quarters Round ~

When the Winter Quarters card appears, set it as the current card and resolve the following phases in order.

1

Victory Check 6.1

If any faction meets both its victory conditions positively, the game ends.

2

Supply 6.2

Out-of-supply units pay 1 R, retreat, or are removed. French must Battle British in West Indies.

3

Resources 6.3

British: Forts + non-Blockaded controlled City Pop. Patriots: Forts + ½ Rebellion-controlled spaces. Indians: ½ Villages. French (post-TOA): FNI + non-Br-controlled City Pop.

4

Support 6.4

British Reward Loyalty (needs Regular + Tory + Control). Patriots Committees of Correspondence (needs Rebellion Control + Patriot piece). Max 2 shifts per space.

5

Redeploy 6.5

Leader change (Gage→Howe→Clinton; Rochambeau→Lauzun; Brant→Cornplanter→Dragging Canoe). British Release Date arrivals. FNI drops 1.

6

Desertion 6.6

Remove 1 in 5 Militia, Continentals, and Tories. Enemies choose first.

7

Reset 6.7

Clear Raid & Propaganda markers. All factions Eligible. Casualties → Available. Flip Militia/WPs Underground.

★

Final Winter 2.4.1, 7.3

Final card: stop at Support Phase. Combine both victory conditions into a single margin; highest margin wins. French need TOA played.

~ Suggested Strategies ~

Each faction has a different lever to pull. These are starting templates — your opponents and the card flow will twist them.

The British — "Hold the Cities, Reward the Loyal"

Your engine is City Population. Lose it and your treasury empties.
  1. Garrison every coastal City early. British Resources scale directly with non-Blockaded City Pop in Winter Quarters. Aim for New York City, Philadelphia, Boston, Charles Town, Savannah.
  2. Build Tories aggressively in adjacent Colonies. Tories are cheap, count as cubes in Battle, and are the only thing Skirmish can't easily wipe out. Get them where Active Opposition isn't yet locked.
  3. Reward Loyalty during Muster, not just in Winter. Mid-campaign Reward Loyalty has no shift cap and clears Propaganda/Raid markers.
  4. Let the Indians do dirty work. Pay for joint Scouts; their War Path & Raid reduce Opposition without spending your own Resources.
  5. Watch the French build-up. Once Available French Regulars + Squadrons + CBC nears 15, the Treaty drops. Use Naval Pressure to suppress FNI before then, and don't let CBC balloon needlessly.
  6. Don't chase Battle if Opposition is already crawling. Often a Garrison + Skirmish does more good than a roll-heavy fight that piles CBC onto your worst victory metric.

The Patriots — "Rally, Rouse, Outlast"

You don't have to hold ground — you only have to make it ungovernable.
  1. Underground Militia is your shield. Don't activate unless you must. Rally builds Militia for cheap; pulling adjacent Militia into a Fort space refreshes them all to Underground.
  2. Build Forts in interior Colonies. They give you supply, double your Rally outputs, and feed your second victory condition (Forts + 3 > Villages).
  3. Rabble-Rouse population centers, not empty land. Each Propaganda marker also blocks the British from cheaply Rewarding Loyalty later.
  4. Use Persuasion as a Resource engine. Three controlled spaces = +3 R every time you fire it.
  5. Time Continentals with French arrival. Continentals are pricey to use defensively, but post-TOA, paired with French Regulars under Rochambeau, they become a hammer.
  6. Mind your Forts vs Villages math. If Indians out-build you on Villages, you can win on Opposition and still lose the second condition. Skirmish/Partisans against Indians when needed.

The Indians — "Many Villages, Few Forts"

You are Royalist by alignment but adversarial to the Patriots specifically.
  1. Build Villages early, especially in Indian Reserve. First Reserve Province is free to Gather. Villages are your victory condition and your Resource engine (½ Villages per Winter Quarters).
  2. Raid Patriot frontier Colonies relentlessly. Each Raid drops Opposition a level and locks the space with a marker. Three Provinces a turn for 3 R is one of the best deals in the game.
  3. Trade with the British when their treasury is fat. Trade can shift British R to you with no risk.
  4. Plunder during Raid for double impact. Removing Patriot R while shifting Opposition cripples Patriot Rallies.
  5. Demand British Scout assistance. Pairs your activation with a free British move; great for projecting into contested Colonies.
  6. You can also lose if the British win. Same side ≠ same victory. Don't over-invest in Cities (you can't enter them) — focus on Province count and Villages relative to Patriot Forts.

The French — "Patience, then Thunder"

Before the Treaty: invisible. After the Treaty: decisive.
  1. Préparer la Guerre every chance you get. Build Available Regulars and Squadrons toward the 15-piece threshold (count includes CBC).
  2. Use Roderigue Hortalez et Cie to bankroll Patriots. Every R you spend gives them R+1. Strong Patriots are the precondition for your win.
  3. French Agent Mobilization in New England. Plant Militia in Quebec / New York / New Hampshire / Massachusetts before TOA to seed Rebellion footprint.
  4. Drop the Treaty of Alliance at maximum leverage. Once played, you can suddenly muster Regulars, place Blockades, and Skirmish. Time it for when Opposition is close to the 10+ margin.
  5. FNI for Resources and denial. Each Blockaded City zeroes British City income, blocks Muster/Garrison/March, and boosts your Resource intake.
  6. Watch the West Indies. You must Battle British there each Winter if both are present — keep Squadrons handy and don't let it become a CRC dump.
  7. Without TOA you cannot win. If the deck threatens to bury TOA, play it before Resources are perfectly set rather than miss the window.
